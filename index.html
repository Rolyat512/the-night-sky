Did you know?

When you look at the Andromeda galaxy (which is million light years away), the light you are seeing took million years to reach. each you. So you are seeing the galaxy as it was million years ago.

Every year the sun evaporates 100,00 cubic miles of water from Earth that weigh 400 trillion tons!

Spare a thought for the constillations that never made it into the official list... these inlcude Machina Electrica (the electricity generator), Officina Typographica (The Printing Office), and Turdus Solitarus (the solitary thrush).

Only one side of the moon ever faces Earth. The moons period of rotation is exactly the same as it's period of orbit.

When Galileo viewed Saturn for the first time throgh a telescope, he described the planet as having "ears". It wa not until 1655 that Christian Huygens suggested the crazy theory that they might be an enormous set of rings around the planet.

Its estimated that the number of stars in the universe is greater than the number of grains of san.

Temperatures on Venus are hot enough to melt lead.

If you could travel at the speed of light (186,000 miles per second) it would take 100,000 years to cross our galaxy.

The Earth is not a sphere! It's actually a "speroid", it is squashed slightly at the poles and bulges out at the equator due to its rotation.

Saturn is not the only planet with rings. Neptune has it's own ring system.
